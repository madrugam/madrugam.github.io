Research

My dissertation work examines sentencing as an authoritative speech act with epistemic and ethical stakes. I argue that sentencing implicitly purports to satisfy a justification threshold analogous to (but distinct from) the trial standard, and that epistemic failures in sentencing constitute moral harms.

Dissertation (in progress) Speech acts, epistemic standards, and the morality of punishment
Working themes Risk assessment; biased evidence; safety/sensitivity; authority and justification
Selected questions What epistemic standard should govern sentencing fact-finding? When do legal pronouncements misfire epistemically? How should error costs shape institutional design?

  • Dialogical learning: structured discussion and cooperative inquiry, not adversarial debate.
  • Writing integration: workshops for thesis construction, revision, and argument clarity.
